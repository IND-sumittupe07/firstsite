In-Depth Treatment Information

Learn about each procedure — from symptoms to full recovery.

🦷 Spine Surgery

Spine surgery at Osteon Clinic focuses on treating disorders of the vertebral column including disc problems, nerve compression, and spinal instability. We offer both open and minimally invasive surgical options tailored to each patient.

Common Symptoms:

  • Severe lower back or neck pain
  • Pain radiating into arms or legs (sciatica)
  • Numbness, tingling, or weakness in limbs
  • Difficulty walking or standing for long periods
  • Loss of bladder/bowel control (in severe cases)

Recovery:

Most patients can return to light activities within 4–6 weeks. Full recovery typically takes 3–6 months with guided physiotherapy.

L4-L5 Disc Surgery

Removal of herniated disc material pressing on spinal nerves at the L4-L5 level.

Microdiscectomy

Minimally invasive removal of disc fragments via a small incision with rapid recovery.

Spinal Fusion

Joining two or more vertebrae to stabilize the spine and eliminate painful motion.

Laminectomy

Removal of the lamina to decompress the spinal canal and relieve nerve pressure.

🔭 Arthroscopy

Arthroscopy is a keyhole surgical procedure that allows the surgeon to examine and treat joint problems using a tiny camera (arthroscope). It significantly reduces recovery time compared to open surgery.

Common Symptoms:

  • Joint pain that doesn't respond to conservative treatment
  • Swelling and stiffness of the joint
  • Catching or locking sensation in the joint
  • Reduced range of motion
  • Suspected ligament or cartilage damage

Recovery:

Most arthroscopic surgeries allow patients to go home the same day. Return to normal activities in 2–6 weeks depending on the joint treated.

Knee Arthroscopy

Diagnosis and treatment of meniscus tears, cartilage damage, and ligament injuries.

Shoulder Arthroscopy

Rotator cuff repair, labral tears, and shoulder impingement through tiny incisions.

Hip Arthroscopy

Treatment of hip impingement and labral tears with minimal recovery time.

Ankle Arthroscopy

Removal of loose bodies and treatment of chronic ankle pain via keyhole surgery.

⚡ ACL Tear Treatment

The anterior cruciate ligament (ACL) is a critical knee stabilizer. ACL tears are common in sports involving jumping, pivoting, and sudden direction changes. Arthroscopic ACL reconstruction restores full knee stability.

Common Symptoms:

  • Loud "pop" at time of injury
  • Severe knee pain and immediate swelling
  • Knee instability — feeling of "giving way"
  • Inability to bear weight on the affected leg
  • Loss of full range of motion

Recovery:

Return to sport typically occurs at 6–9 months post-surgery with a structured rehabilitation program.

Arthroscopic Reconstruction

Using hamstring or patellar tendon graft to replace the torn ACL via keyhole technique.

Graft Options

Hamstring autograft, patellar tendon, or cadaver allograft based on patient needs.

Rehabilitation Protocol

Structured 6-month physiotherapy program for full return to sports activities.

Sports Return Planning

Personalized sport-specific training to safely return athletes to full competition.

🦵 Knee Injury Treatment

Knee injuries range from ligament sprains to severe arthritis requiring replacement. We offer a complete spectrum of knee care from conservative management to advanced surgical intervention.

Common Conditions:

  • Meniscus tears (medial and lateral)
  • Knee osteoarthritis (mild to severe)
  • Patellofemoral pain syndrome
  • Cartilage damage and bone-on-bone wear
  • Tibial plateau fractures

Recovery:

Knee replacement patients walk within 24 hours. Full recovery takes 3–6 months with physiotherapy support.

Total Knee Replacement

Complete resurfacing of knee joint with durable metal and plastic implants.

Partial Knee Replacement

Replacement of only the affected compartment of the knee for faster recovery.

Meniscus Repair

Arthroscopic stitching or removal of torn meniscal tissue.

Cartilage Restoration

Techniques to regrow or replace damaged knee cartilage for younger patients.

💪 Shoulder Injury Treatment

Shoulder problems are common and can significantly limit daily activities. Our shoulder specialists use advanced arthroscopic techniques to address a wide range of conditions with minimal downtime.

Common Conditions:

  • Rotator cuff tears (partial and complete)
  • Shoulder impingement syndrome
  • SLAP (labrum) tears
  • Frozen shoulder (adhesive capsulitis)
  • Shoulder dislocation and instability

Recovery:

Most shoulder arthroscopy patients begin physiotherapy within 1–2 weeks. Full recovery in 3–6 months.

Rotator Cuff Repair

Arthroscopic repair of torn rotator cuff tendons for pain relief and strength restoration.

Shoulder Stabilization

Bankart repair to prevent recurrent shoulder dislocations.

Acromioplasty

Removal of bone spurs causing impingement and chronic shoulder pain.

Shoulder Replacement

Total or partial shoulder replacement for severe arthritis cases.

🩹 Ligament Injury Treatment

Ligament injuries can range from mild sprains to complete tears requiring surgical reconstruction. Our specialists are experienced in treating all grades of ligament injuries across multiple joints.

Common Conditions:

  • ACL (anterior cruciate ligament) tears
  • PCL (posterior cruciate ligament) tears
  • MCL/LCL sprains and tears
  • Ankle ligament sprains (Grade I-III)
  • Multi-ligament knee injuries

Recovery:

Minor ligament sprains heal in 4–8 weeks. Surgical reconstructions require 6–12 months of rehabilitation.

PCL Reconstruction

Arthroscopic graft-based reconstruction of the posterior cruciate ligament.

MCL Repair

Surgical or bracing treatment for medial collateral ligament injuries.

Ankle Ligament Repair

Brostrom procedure for chronic ankle instability after repeated sprains.

PRP Therapy

Platelet-rich plasma injections to accelerate ligament healing non-surgically.
